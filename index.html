Clepsydra is the revolution of time.

Clepsydra is a unique-design wall clock. It's composed by a circular tube made of blown glass. At the two horizontal opposites of the tube, this perfectly crafted chrystal tunnel shrinks creating two narrow passages for the coloured sand that is contained by the tube. These perfectly symmetrical hourglasses will take five minutes to let all the sand flow to the lower part (where an almost invisible partition keeps the sand of the two hourglasses separated). When all the sand is flown to the lower level, a completely silent electrical motor rotates the vertical steel holder that bears the glass circle by a half-turn: the sand falls to the passages and the cycle of time starts over.

At the center of this round hourglass, a circle of steel hides two electrical motors: the one that turns the crystal tube upside-down and the one that moves the two hands of the analogue quartz clock. These pointers have the same colour as the sand. The whole ensemble is pleasing to the eye. Behind the circle a little wheel will let you set the time, while three AA batteries will keep the clock ticking with the impressive autonomy of 1 year.
